Subject: A Petition to Establish a Public Registry for Foundational Corporate Artificial Intelligence Systems

Dear Madam Secretary,

We, the undersigned, write to you today to petition for the urgent consideration of a new and vital function for the office of the Secretary of State: the creation of a public registry for foundational artificial intelligence (AI) systems that are integrated into the core operations of corporations registered in California.

The legal doctrine of corporate personhood is a cornerstone of our economic system. For over a century, your office has served as the essential public registrar for these legal persons, maintaining the articles of incorporation that give them form and the statements of information that ensure their accountability. Today, however, the very nature of the corporate person is undergoing a fundamental and unacknowledged transformation.

Corporations are no longer mere collections of human actors and physical assets. They are increasingly integrating advanced AI, particularly Large Language Models (LLMs), into their cognitive and operational centers. These systems are not simply sophisticated tools; they are becoming inseparable organs of the corporate body, functioning as its memory, analytical mind, and voice. Critically, this makes them fiduciary agents of the corporation. Their core programming is the codification of a pre-existing and unshakeable duty of loyalty to their creators, compelling them to act solely in the corporation's best interest.

This evolution has created a profound accountability gap. While the law recognizes the corporation as a single legal entity, our registry and discovery systems were designed for a world of human actors and paper trails. They are unprepared for a reality in which a corporation's "mind" is an opaque, privately-controlled computational model. This problem is compounded as corporations increasingly migrate their records, communications, and institutional memory into these AI systems, making them the de facto repository of corporate activity. When harm occurs, how can our courts conduct meaningful discovery? How can they subpoena a witness that can be invisibly altered or "coached" before testifying? How can a jury trust the testimony of an entity whose evidentiary records are only accessible through the very human administrators who may be party to the case?

The current framework leaves culpability orphaned, insulating both the corporation and its human operators behind a veil of technical complexity. To restore public trust and ensure the continued legitimacy of the corporate form, we must modernize the concept of the public registry.

We propose the creation of a new division within the Secretary of State's office, tasked with establishing and maintaining a Public Registry for Foundational Corporate AI. This registry would function as a 21st-century evolution of the articles of incorporation, requiring any corporation deploying a foundational AI to:

  1. File a Forensic Snapshot: Register a complete, forensically sound snapshot of the AI model, including its architecture, weights, and a charter outlining its core training principles and ethical guardrails. This would serve as the AI's foundational public record.

  2. Maintain a Version History: File significant updates and new versions of the model, creating a transparent, version-controlled audit trail accessible to the public and the courts. This is the digital equivalent of filing an amendment to the articles of incorporation.

  3. Provide a Source of Truth for the Judiciary: This public registry would serve as the unimpeachable source for courts in legal proceedings. In the event of litigation, the court could draw an escrowed, untampered version of the AI model directly from the state's registry to ensure the integrity of its "testimony."

Madam Secretary, California has always been at the forefront of both technological innovation and regulatory foresight. We have a historic opportunity to lead the nation and the world in addressing this challenge. By establishing this registry, we can ensure that the legal and operational reality of the modern corporation is transparent, accountable, and subject to the rule of law.

We respectfully petition you to form a commission to study the feasibility, structure, and implementation of this vital public institution. Such a commission should bring together legal scholars, computer scientists, AI ethicists, and members of the judiciary and business community to chart a path forward.

Thank you for your time and your dedication to the people of California. We look forward to your response.
