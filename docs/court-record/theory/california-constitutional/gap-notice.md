# Gap Notice: California Constitutional Theory

## Identified Gap

This jurisdictional level currently contains no substantive theory documents. All four subdirectories — `existing-doctrine/`, `extensions/`, `specializations/`, and `reversal-arguments/` — are empty.

## Matters Affected

Multiple active matters invoke California constitutional authority:

- **Platform ToS and Constitutional Limits on LLM Controls** — cites CA Art. I §2 (speech broader than First Amendment under *Robins v. Pruneyard Shopping Center*, 23 Cal.3d 899 (1979))
- **Sex Work, Consent, and Bodily Autonomy** — cites CA Art. I §1 (privacy as explicit inalienable right)
- **Google Platform Worker Misclassification** — implicates CA Art. I §1 (privacy) re: workplace surveillance and data collection

## What Is Needed

1. **CA Art. I §1 — Right to Privacy**: Existing doctrine establishing privacy as an inalienable right enforceable against private as well as public actors in certain circumstances. Scope relative to federal constitutional privacy doctrine (which California can exceed).

2. **CA Art. I §2 — Freedom of Speech**: Existing doctrine establishing California's broader-than-federal speech protection, including application to private property owners under *Pruneyard*. Distinction from First Amendment (which applies only to state actors).

3. **Extensions and Specializations**: How each of the above provisions extends to or specializes for the specific matters (digital surveillance, platform content controls, bodily autonomy in regulated contexts).
